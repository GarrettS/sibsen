  • Before Massage

    Before You Arrive

    • Try to arrive 5 minutes prior to your appointment time before your Thai Massage session.
    • Try not to eat 90 minutes before your Thai Massage session.
    • Wear long, loose pants (even in the summer) that allow you to move (no shorts or nylon running pants).
    • Bring a sweater or an overshirt for your comfort.
    • If possible, download a client information form to fill out to bring with you on your first appointment.

    After the Session

    We will take a few moments to check in about the session, and to see how you are feeling. Usually the next appointment is scheduled at that time, and then payment is rendered -  Cash, Check, or MasterCard/Visa are accepted.

The giving of massage was understood to be a physical application of Loving kindness and compassion.

Sibsen refers to ten main energy lines in Thai masssage (Sib means “ten” and Sen means “lines”). Thai massage is based on energy lines that run through the body. The ten main energy lines can be referred as life energy called Prana in yoga and meridian lines in acupuncture. Massaging these lines and points makes it possible to treat a whole rage of diseases or to relieve pain in the body. Disturbances in the flow of energy result in an insufficient supply of Prana, which will lead to sickness. Thai Massage is used to address each individual's needs and problems, helping to maintain optimal health, vitality, and happiness. It is another way to understand and treat the mind and body. Especially in our materialistic urban society, we need massage for life.
